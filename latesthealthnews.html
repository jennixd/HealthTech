Community Health Champions recognized for Promoting well-being in Singapore.

Date Posted: February 10, 2024
Writer: Healthier SG

In a significant event, the Sihat Bersama JKJU celebration brought together leaders, community partners, and members to champion the physical and mental well-being of the Malay/Muslim community.

The Jaga Kesihatan, Jaga Ummah (JKJU) network, introduced by the Health Promotion Board (HPB) in 2017, has seen remarkable growth, expanding from five mosques to 100 active partners, including Malay Activity Executive Committees and various organizations. The network has played a crucial role in engaging over 40,000 Malay/Muslim residents in healthy lifestyle activities, underscoring the collective dedication to fostering healthier habits.

The commitment to community health is further emphasized through the newly established focus area on community health under M3, led by SPS Rahayu. The focus areas include supporting healthy aging, improving screening rates, adopting a healthy lifestyle, reducing smoking prevalence, and enhancing mental well-being.

In recognition of outstanding contributions, 99 recipients, including M3@Towns, mosques, MAECs, and individuals, were honored for actively promoting and making healthy living accessible for the broader Malay/Muslim community. This marked a significant increase from the previous JKJU event in 2022, showcasing the growing engagement in adopting healthier lifestyles.

Notable initiatives, such as the Saham Kesihatan programme named Gaya Sihat by M3@Marsiling-Yew Tee, exemplify the collaborative efforts to enhance ground-up initiatives. The programme, featuring weekly physical activities and collaborations with healthcare institutions and religious centers, highlights the potential of tapping into available resources.

The event emphasized the importance of a community-wide effort, leveraging close working relationships and the spirit of Gotong Royong (working together). Leaders encouraged small changes in daily lives, such as brisk walks and healthier food choices, to instill healthier habits.

In conclusion, the celebration not only honored exemplary efforts but also aimed to inspire a broader adoption of healthy living as a way of life for the Malay/Muslim community in Singapore. The event concluded with heartfelt congratulations to all recipients and a call to continue making health a top priority.
